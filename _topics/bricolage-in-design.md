## Definition

**Bricolage in design** is creating through resourceful recombination of what is already at hand: materials, components, tools, skills, precedents, relationships, and partial ideas. The bricoleur does not begin with an unconstrained specification and procure ideal resources; the available repertoire actively shapes both the problem and the solution.

> Our stance: bricolage is not a euphemism for sloppy improvisation. Good bricolage is disciplined responsiveness under real resource conditions. It is a mode of design that optimization-centered accounts routinely undervalue.

The concept comes from Claude Lévi-Strauss's contrast between the bricoleur and an idealized engineer. In practice, designers move between both modes: planning and making-do, abstraction and material encounter, optimization and opportunistic recombination.

## Making do by design

1. Inventory the resources, fragments, capabilities, and relationships available.
2. Reinterpret what those resources might do beyond their original purpose.
3. Combine and test partial arrangements.
4. Let discoveries reframe the problem and reveal new resources.
5. Stabilize a provisional solution, while retaining the history and constraints that shaped it.

Bricolage may be physical, digital, organizational, or methodological. A researcher can also assemble methods responsively, but should still explain why the combination is coherent.

### Constraint as creative material
Bricolage is useful under scarcity, time pressure, uncertainty, local adaptation, repair, entrepreneurship, prototyping, and community-based design. It also describes ordinary expert practice: designers rarely begin from nothing, and existing artifacts carry knowledge.

It should not be romanticized. “Use what is at hand” can reproduce unsafe workarounds or inequitable resource conditions unless constraints and consequences are made visible.

### Making do is not lowering the bar
- **Equating bricolage with random tinkering.** The repertoire is heterogeneous, but the recombination is purposive.
- **Treating constraints only as deficits.** Available resources can generate possibilities as well as limit them.
- **Erasing origin and ownership.** Reuse still requires attention to credit, consent, maintenance, and cultural meaning.
- **Opposing bricolage and engineering too literally.** Real design moves between planned and situated modes.
- **Calling an incoherent method mix “methodological bricolage.”** Flexibility does not remove the need for rigor.

## Resources

- <strong><a href="https://press.uchicago.edu/ucp/books/book/chicago/W/bo19238845.html">Wild Thought: A New Translation of <em>La Pensée sauvage</em></a></strong> — Claude Lévi-Strauss (1962; new translation 2021). Introduces the bricoleur as one who creates with a finite, heterogeneous repertoire at hand.
- **[Design as Bricolage: Anthropology Meets Design Thinking](https://doi.org/10.1016/S0142-694X(98)00044-1)** — Panos Louridas (1999), <em>Design Studies</em>. Develops bricolage as a descriptive account of both traditional and contemporary design.
- **[Creating Something From Nothing](https://doi.org/10.2189/asqu.2005.50.3.329)** — Ted Baker and Reed Nelson (2005), <em>Administrative Science Quarterly</em>. Explains entrepreneurial bricolage as resource construction under constraint.
- **[Discursive Engineering Design](https://www.designsociety.org/publication/40643/)** — a Design Society paper that directly revisits the engineer–bricoleur contrast.
- **[Fitting Squares Into Round Holes](https://www.designsociety.org/publication/39595/)** — examines corporate fabrication labs as sites of bricolage and cross-functional making.
- **[Racism Untaught](https://racismuntaught.com/)** — Terresa Moses and Lisa Mercer offer a design-research framework for recognizing racialized artifacts and systems; it is a strong model for making context and inherited materials explicit.
- **[REID Lab](https://engineering.purdue.edu/reidlab/)** — Tahira Reid Smith's work on socially and culturally relevant design broadens what counts as a legitimate design constraint and resource.
